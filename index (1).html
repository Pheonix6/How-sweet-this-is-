KIT KAT

History

KitKat is a chocolate-covered wafer bar confection that was created by the British confectionery company, Rowntree's, in 1935. It was first launched as "Rowntree's Chocolate Crisp" in the United Kingdom, and later, in 1937, it was renamed to "KitKat Chocolate Crisp". The name "KitKat" is said to have been inspired by the Kit-Cat Club, an 18th-century London club for literary men that was known for its tasty mutton pies. The bar originally came in one flavor, milk chocolate, and its four-finger shape was introduced in 1958. In 1973, Rowntree's was acquired by the Swiss food company Nestlé, which still produces KitKat today. The chocolate bar has become one of Nestlé's most successful brands, with variations such as dark chocolate, white chocolate, and different flavors like strawberry, matcha, and cheesecake. KitKat has become a cultural icon in many countries, with the four-finger design and the "Have a break, have a KitKat" slogan being recognized around the world. In Japan, KitKat is particularly popular, with over 300 different flavors, including green tea, wasabi, and sake. In recent years, KitKat has also been involved in sustainability efforts, such as sourcing cocoa from sustainable sources and reducing waste in its manufacturing processes.

Ingredients

The ingredients of a classic KitKat bar vary slightly by region, but generally include:

  • Sugar
  • Wheat flour
  • Whole milk powder
  • Cocoa butter
  • Vegetable fat (palm oil, shea oil)
  • Cocoa mass
  • Emulsifiers (soy lecithin, polyglycerol polyricinoleate)
  • Baking powder (sodium bicarbonate)
  • Salt
  • Flavorings (natural and artificial)
    • Note that the specific ingredients and nutritional information may vary based on the size and flavor of the KitKat bar, as well as the region where it's sold. For example, in some regions, KitKat bars may contain nuts or other allergens, so it's important to check the packaging carefully if you have any dietary restrictions.
